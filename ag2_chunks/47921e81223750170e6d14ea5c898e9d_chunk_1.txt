<h3 id="header-9">IDEA Guide: Offline participation</h3>
<p>To invite team members who do not have their own email address or have limited internet access to an IDEA guide survey, you can create vouchers to give these so-called offline users access to the survey and the IDEA Guide voting page.</p>
<h4 id="header-11">Configuration</h4>
<p>Click on the <strong>Invitation</strong> tab of the guide configuration of your IDEA guide and select an e-mail template for the voucher codes.</p>
<p><img src="https://discoverxi-supportdesk.zendesk.com/hc/article_attachments/21036869207442" alt="Offline users e-mail settings.png"></p>
<p> </p>
<p>Then activate the "Allow offline participations" option and define the number of possible offline participants (max. 100). You also need to select a panel group to which the offline participants will be assigned.</p>
<p><img src="https://discoverxi-supportdesk.zendesk.com/hc/article_attachments/21036864015378" alt="Offline users participants.png"></p>
<p> </p>
<p> </p>
<p>The vouchers include the following information:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Wave topic</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Manager name</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Wave creation date</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>QR code and URL</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>You can also change the logo of the vouchers.</p>
<p><img src="https://discoverxi-supportdesk.zendesk.com/hc/article_attachments/21036863636882" alt="Offline users voucher.png"></p>
<p>Participants who are invited via vouchers will not get access to the <em>Action Board</em> to see results of the process.</p>
<h3 id="header-13">ME Guide: Process details after process ended</h3>
<p>From now on, the participant rates of the various feedback groups are available to the guide initiator even after the process has been completed. This makes it possible to draw better conclusions about the reasons for a failed survey.</p>
