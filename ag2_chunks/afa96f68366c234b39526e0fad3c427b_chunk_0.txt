<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">This article covers the process of testing and activating a project in EFS, as well as exporting results. <p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">This guide focuses on the final stages of setting up a project in EFS, including activation, testing, and data export. It also covers important aspects of access control and mail template types for multi-source feedback.</p>

<h2 id="h_01HGKSMPRT0QJQX8YJXQX3YDWM" class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">Activating the Project</h2>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">Before starting the feedback process, you need to adjust the basic settings of the project in the Survey menu.</p>
<h3 id="h_01HGKSMPRTV5YJXQBQJXF1QXCWP" class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">Step 1: Specify Project Duration</h3>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">Specify the beginning and end of the project. Confirm with Save.</p>
<h3 id="h_01HGKSMPRTZBJ8XQJXF1QXCWN9C" class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">Step 2: Activate the Project</h3>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">In the "Status" line, you'll see that the project has been created and is currently in the status "survey compiled". Click on the active button to change the status to "active".</p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify"><img src="https://discoverxi-supportdesk.zendesk.com/hc/article_attachments/21015041292818"></p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify"><strong>Note:</strong> The interval specified in the survey menu is the field time of the project itself. Individual feedback waves may have shorter field times, but these must be inside the overall project field time.</p>
<h2 id="h_01HGKSMPRT5YJXQBQJXF1QXCWN" class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">Testing the Project</h2>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">Before entering the field, you need to test both the questionnaire and the feedback center.</p>
<h3 id="h_01HGKSMPRTZ5YJXQBQJXF1QXCWQ" class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">Testing the Questionnaire in Preview Mode</h3>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">In the "Distribution" section of the survey menu, click on the Questionnaire Preview icon and check the survey from the point of view of a participant. Go through the questionnaire and try whether filters, hiding conditions, and plausibility checks really work.</p>
<h3 id="h_01HGKSMPRU3BJ8XQJXF1QXCWN0D" class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">Testing Questionnaire and Feedback Center with Test Participants</h3>
<ol class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">
<li>Create a test wave in the menu Participant administration → Waves.</li>
<li>Create participants manually and define a feedback constellation, or import a list of test participants into the test wave.</li>
<li>To access the questionnaire from the perspective of a feedback provider:
<ul>
<li>Open the feedback provider overview: Participant administration → {Selected wave} → {Selected focus person}.</li>
<li>For the desired test feedback provider, click on the code link in the "Code" column.</li>
<li>The questionnaire will open, allowing you to check wildcard replacement, routing, etc.</li>
</ul>
</li>

<ul>
<li>Open the focus persons overview: Participant administration → {Selected wave}.</li>
