<h2 id="ACL-rights" class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify" data-renderer-start-pos="26211"><strong data-renderer-mark="true">ACL rights</strong></h2>
<h2 id="h_01J35XYX20MDBA9Y6N3QATN9AY" class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify" data-renderer-start-pos="26211"><u style="font-size: 15px;" data-renderer-mark="true"><img src="https://discoverxi-supportdesk.zendesk.com/hc/article_attachments/21015136225554"></u></h2>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify" data-renderer-start-pos="26211"><span class="wysiwyg-underline">There are usually a number of staff working in EFS installations, often in larger staff groups.</span></p>
<ul class="ak-ul wysiwyg-text-align-justify" data-indent-level="1">
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="26325">In addition to project management, large numbers of additional users are often involved in large survey projects or the administration of panels. There are, for example, users that specialize in questionnaire programming, while other users are responsible for the evaluation of questionnaires.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="26626">In collective installations, users from different clients work completely separately on their respective projects.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="26744">Some users may not be granted access to the admin area. Instead, separate logins such as EFS Survey Status and EFS Translator Interface provide restricted access to the information and functions their work requires.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify" data-renderer-start-pos="26963"><span class="wysiwyg-underline">EFS allows you to effectively configure and control the access rights of all these staff</span></p>
<ul class="ak-ul wysiwyg-text-align-justify" data-indent-level="1">
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="27055">Instead of configuring the access rights for each individual user, you define teams: These reflect the respective rights and duties of entire user groups with identical responsibilities. Subsequently, you allocate the actual users to the user roles.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="27308">The various ways of accessing and changing information and functions are mapped in a dedicated rights system. Thus, you can not only define which information and functions a team may generally access, but also which individual objects (e.g. specific projects)</p>
</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="Assignement" class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify" data-renderer-start-pos="27571"><strong data-renderer-mark="true">Assignment</strong></h3>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify" data-renderer-start-pos="27584">The particular access options of users in <em data-renderer-mark="true">EFS</em> are controlled via assignment to one or more teams:</p>
<ul class="ak-ul wysiwyg-text-align-justify" data-indent-level="1">
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="27685">The configuration of the teams reflects the respective rights and responsibilities of entire staff groups with identical responsibilities.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="27827">Users can be members of several teams.</p>
<ul class="ak-ul" data-indent-level="2">
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="27869">The ACL rights of his teams are added up, i.e. he may access all information and functions that at least one of his teams has access to. If, for example, one of his teams holds write rights for <em data-renderer-mark="true">ct42partadm</em>, i.e. it can create participants in the participant administration, while another of his team has only read rights, he can create participants.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="28222">With regard to individual objects, he also has the widest access that is configured for one of his teams, i.e. if one of his teams holds write rights for project x while another team only has read rights, he has write rights.</p>
</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="28453">Each user belongs to one primary team.</p>

<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="28495">The primary team
