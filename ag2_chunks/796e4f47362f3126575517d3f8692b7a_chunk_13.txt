<h3 id="collections" class="sc-lhVmIH iJLaKb wysiwyg-text-align-justify"><strong>Collections</strong></h3>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify"><span style="color: #091e42;">The use of </span><strong>Collections</strong><span style="color: #091e42;"><strong> </strong>in the mapping depends on whether you fill a single shape cell with the collection or whether you use the collection in the Loop mode to fill the columns or rows of the selected table. First, you will learn about shape cell mapping. You map the </span>Dimension and Unit collections<span style="color: #091e42;"> created under the sub-navigation </span><strong>Data</strong><span style="color: #091e42;">.</span></p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify"><strong><span style="color: #091e42;">Dimension and Unit collections</span></strong></p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">If you select the element<strong> Collections</strong>, the dimension or unit collections you created are available. You click either on the created <strong>Dimenison collection</strong> or on the created<strong>Unit collection</strong>. The meta names you have assigned are displayed.</p>
<ul class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">
<li>You fill a cell of the shape with a member of the dimension or unit collection.</li>
<li>The members of the dimension or unit collection are subject to a sort order that you either define yourself or that you define in <strong>Sort order</strong> under the tab<strong> Extras</strong> on the sub-navigation field <strong>Data</strong>.</li>
<li>Using the element<strong> Item</strong>, you can take a member from this sequence.</li>
<li>If you do not define a sort order, the item x corresponds to the collection member set in place of the sequence.</li>
<li>If you define a sort order, it is not possible to see which member of the collection is mapped. It is the member who, by virtue of its value, occupies that position x in the order of its members.</li>
<li>The sequence is defined by the sort order.</li>
</ul>
