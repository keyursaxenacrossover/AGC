<h3 id="page-title-and-url" class="sc-eNNmBn lmEGgd wysiwyg-text-align-justify"><strong>The page title and URL</strong></h3>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify"><span style="color: #091e42;">The page title and URL relation works just as it does with the portal‘s title and URL relation.</span></p>
<ul class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">
<li>To change a page‘s name, click into the respective field, delete the entry, and type in a new name.</li>
<li>Press Enter or click any place outside the name field to save your changes.</li>
</ul>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">The URL will adapt accordingly if it has not been altered manually before. <span style="color: #333333;">The URL will adjust to any title changes you make but be aware that once you manually edit the page‘s URL it will no longer adapt automatically to any future changes of the page name.</span></p>
<h3 id="page-options" class="sc-eNNmBn lmEGgd wysiwyg-text-align-justify"><strong>Page options</strong></h3>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify"><span style="color: #091e42;">You can decide whether your page will be visible to everyone (public), or whether you want to restrict access to logged-in users, or to groups. </span>You click on the pen icon and assign access permissions to one of the three options:</p>
<ul class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">
<li>Public</li>
<li>logged-in users</li>
<li>Group members</li>
</ul>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">If you select <strong>Group members</strong>, a list of all created groups is displayed. <span style="color: #091e42;">Only panelist accounts meeting the criteria selected will now have access to the page‘s contents. <span style="color: #333333;">The default setting is </span><strong>logged-in users</strong><span style="color: #333333;">.</span></span></p>
<h3 id="searching-for-pages" class="sc-eNNmBn lmEGgd wysiwyg-text-align-justify"><strong>Searching for pages</strong></h3>
<p class="sc-eNNmBn lmEGgd wysiwyg-text-align-justify">Use the incremental search function just above the button <strong>+ New page</strong> to search for a specific page. You type in the name or part of the name of the page into the <strong>Search page</strong> entry field.</p>
