<h2 id="creating-and-managing-topics" class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify"><strong>Creating and Managing Topics</strong></h2>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">A forum usually contains different topics for discussion, which are specified by the administrator. The panelists can start threads related to a topic and post contributions, but cannot create topics themselves.</p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify"><strong>List of topics</strong></p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">To view a forum’s topics, just click on its title in the forum overview. Alternatively, use the <strong>Edit Topics</strong> icon.</p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">The list of topics contains all topics of the selected forum with description, number of threads, field period, and status.</p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify"><strong>Editing options</strong></p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">If you click on the <strong>Add Topic</strong> button, the dialog for creating a new topic is opened.<br>By clicking on the title of a topic, you will get to the individual posts.</p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">Use the icons in the “Actions” column to edit individual topics:</p>
<ul class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">
<li>
<strong>Edit</strong>: Opens the dialog for changing topic properties. For detailed information on the properties.</li>
<li>
<strong>Edit posts</strong>: Opens the list of posts.</li>
<li>
<strong>Copy</strong>: Copies the selected topic. The posts belonging to the topic are not copied, and this is usually not advisable.</li>
<li>
<strong>Export posts</strong>: Triggers an export of all posts of the topic.</li>
<li>
<strong>Delete</strong>: The topic is deleted after a confirmation prompt. Please note that all data are irrevocably lost in the process. If you no longer need a certain topic, we recommend exporting the posts before deleting them.</li>
</ul>
