<h3 id="h_01HGKSMPRS7YJGQX3YBVHXKP6N" class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">Simple Search</h3>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify"><strong>Executing a Search Query:</strong></p>
<ol class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">
<li>Go to People → Panelists administration.</li>
<li>In the “Search text” field, enter a term or part of a term (e.g., panelist's name).</li>
<li>Click on <strong>Search</strong>.</li>
<li>If the term is found in a panelist’s data record, the panelist will be displayed (deleted panelists are ignored).</li>
<li>Click on the panelist's name to open the detail view.</li>
</ol>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify"><strong>How the Search Function Works:</strong></p>
<ul class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">
<li>Ignores upper and lower case.</li>
<li>Excludes the following fields: "WWW" (u_www), "street" (u_street), "place" (u_city), "PLZ" (u_zip), and "phone number" (u_phone).</li>
<li>Returns all entries containing the entered term.</li>
</ul>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify"><strong>Note:</strong></p>
<ul class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">
<li>Large results may not display all panelists on the first page. Use the &gt; (Next page) and &lt; (Previous page) links to scroll through the results.</li>
</ul>
