Survey messages can be exported and imported into another EFS installation).</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">In the following, you will first learn how to define and edit the survey messages for your project and, if necessary, how to store them for further use elsewhere. After that, you will be introduced to the library.</p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify"><strong>Viewing survey messages of the project</strong></p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">You will find the survey messages of your project in the <strong>Projects</strong> → {Selected project} → <strong>Project properties</strong> → <strong>Survey messages</strong> menu.</p>
<ul class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">
<li>
<p>The entry dialog normally shows the survey messages that are currently defined for the default language of the selected project. Please note: The button labels defined in this menu are used both for HTML buttons and for graphical form elements.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>In multilingual projects, you define a different set of survey messages for each language: use the <strong>View</strong> feature to additionally display these messages.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify"><strong>Editing survey messages of the project</strong></p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">There are the following options, depending on whether you want to edit individual messages or the entire set:</p>
<ul class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">
<li>
<p>If you merely want to change individual messages, simply overwrite the existing text and click on <strong>Save</strong>.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>If you want to replace the existing messages entirely with another set of messages from the library, please proceed as follows: Specify the correct target language in the field “Which language do you want to fill with text elements from the library?”. Then, specify the desired set in the field “Text elements you wish to copy from the survey messages library:” and confirm by clicking on <strong>Apply</strong>.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify"><strong>Storing survey messages of the project in the library</strong></p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">If you want to use the messages of your project in other projects, too, you can store them in the library. Click on the button <strong>Save survey messages in the library</strong>. In the subsequent dialog, enter your name and description. You also have to specify the survey messages of which project language you want to be stored. Click on <strong>Save</strong>. The messages will be added to the menu <strong>Libraries → Survey messages</strong>, where they will be available independent of the project.</p>
