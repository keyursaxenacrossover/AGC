”, newly arriving prospective participants will be denied access with the message “No further participants can be admitted”.</p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">In contrast, participants who are already active can fill out their questionnaire unhindered. In most usage scenarios, a sudden closure of their sessions in the middle of the questionnaire would be counterproductive. For this reason, participant numbers may exceed the defined limit, especially if many respondents participate at the same time in large surveys.)</p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify"><strong>Activating the participation counter</strong></p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">Switch to the <strong>Project </strong>→ {<strong>Selected project</strong>} → <strong>Project information. </strong>Click on the slider symbol (<strong>Set target</strong>) next to the participation counter in the status box. Enter into the text field “Number of additional necessary survey participations” the number of participants required from this point in time. The participation counter counts backwards from this value. You also have the option of counting respondents with the tester status. To do this tick the corresponding checkbox. You have the option of counting quoted and screened out participants (disposition code 36 and 37). Complete the work process by clicking on <strong>Save</strong>.</p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify"><strong>Editing the rejection message</strong></p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">The survey message that is displayed when the number of participants is too high can be found in the <strong>Project properties </strong>→<strong> Survey messages </strong>menu. The title of the message is “The survey is in status 'no further participations'”, a suitable message would be “No further participants allowed.”</p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify"><strong>Informing respondents about the current state of the participation counter</strong></p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">The current value of the participation counter can be inserted into project-related mails with the wildcard #participants_left#.</p>

<h3 id="accessible" class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify"><strong>Accessible</strong></h3>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">All standard question types are accessible. The respective page can be captured, structured and read by a screen reader software and can be controlled completely by keyboard (using tab and arrow keys). A mouse is not necessary.</p>
<ul class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">
<li>
<p>Complex types of questions (hotspots, images) are not necessarily designed to be accessible, since they are dependent, for example, on further descriptions of the visible image by the survey creator.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Responsive Layout 3 offers significant improvements in the area of accessibility.</p>
</li>
</ul>
