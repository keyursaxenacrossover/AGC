er-start-pos="1441">Now that your report has been created, you can start building out the structure.</p>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="1523">The <strong data-renderer-mark="true">Sources</strong> area on the left-hand side contains all available source data (questions, survey data and possibly participant data), recodes, structuring elements such as filters for restricting the base, and split dimensions for creating contingency tables. The structure of the report is built in the <strong data-renderer-mark="true">Structure</strong> area and individual tables and diagrams can be opened and edited in the <strong data-renderer-mark="true">Workpad</strong> area.</p>
<h4 id="Dividing-the-Report-into-Chapters" data-renderer-start-pos="1920"><strong data-renderer-mark="true">Dividing the Report into Chapters</strong></h4>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="1955">In the<strong data-renderer-mark="true"> Structure</strong> area, for every new report a first chapter is available by default. You can add further chapters, if required. In the finished report, these chapters are displayed differently depending on the selected export format. The following formats are available:</p>
<ul class="ak-ul" data-indent-level="1">
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="2229">HTML reports: the chapters are displayed in the navigation bar of the report. The contents of each chapter are summarized on an HTML page.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="2371">Excel reports: the contents of a chapter are each summarized on a tab.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="2445">PPT reports and PDFs generated on the basis of PPT reports: a new PPT slide starts at the beginning of each chapter.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="2565"><strong data-renderer-mark="true">To rename the existing chapter and add further chapters:</strong></p>
<ul class="ak-ul" data-indent-level="1">
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="2626">To rename the first chapter, click on the corresponding chapter in the<strong data-renderer-mark="true"> Structure</strong> area.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="2716">Then, click on the pencil icon.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="2751">Enter a new title and click on <strong data-renderer-mark="true">Save</strong>.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="2791">To add a new chapter, click on the plus icon in the <strong data-renderer-mark="true">Structure</strong> area.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="2862">Enter a title and click on <strong data-renderer-mark="true">Save</strong>.</p>
</li>
</ul>

<h4 id="Evaluating-Questions-by-Means-of-Tables" data-renderer-start-pos="2903"><strong data-renderer-mark="true">Evaluating Questions by Means of Tables</strong></h4>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="2944">Now it’s time to fill the created chapters of your report with content. With Reporting+, you can easily and in just a few steps display the questions from your survey project in the form of tables for evaluation purposes.</p>
<ul class="ak-ul" data-indent-level="1">
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="3170">In the <strong data-renderer-mark="true">Sources </strong>area, click on the plus symbol next to <strong data-renderer-mark="true">Questions</strong>.         <img src="https://discoverxi-supportdesk.zendesk.com/hc/article_attachments/21015166378386"></p>
</li>
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="3170">All available questions from your survey are displayed. Drag and drop the desired question into the <strong data-renderer-mark="true">Structure</strong> area and into the desired chapter. To do this, click on the question, hold down the mouse button and drag the question to the right onto the chapter while still holding down the mouse button. The bar above the question and the chapter itself are now displayed in green and you can release the mouse button. You can also select several questions at the same time and then drag them together into the <strong data-renderer-mark="true">Structure</strong> area.</p>
