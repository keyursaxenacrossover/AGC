<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify" data-renderer-start-pos="9798"><strong data-renderer-mark="true">Reactivating suspended accounts</strong></p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify" data-renderer-start-pos="9831">A suspended account can be reactivated prematurely by the system administrator (root team) or by a user with write rights to “groupadmin”. By clicking on the red-marked end date for the suspension period, the suspension will be reverted.</p>
<h3 id="Delegating-the-administration-of-user-accounts" class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify" data-renderer-start-pos="10070"><strong data-renderer-mark="true">Delegating the administration of user accounts</strong></h3>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify" data-renderer-start-pos="10118">You can delegate the administration of the user accounts of a specific organization. This is the purpose of the ACL right <em data-renderer-mark="true">org_groupadmin</em>: If you assign this right, instead of the more general right <em data-renderer-mark="true">groupadmin</em>, to a user team, its members can access all user accounts of their own organization.</p>
<ul class="ak-ul wysiwyg-text-align-justify" data-indent-level="1">
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="10415">With read rights, they can view the account data of the users of their own organization.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="10507">With write rights, they can manage the users of their own organization (e.g. extend accounts, edit account data, or delete accounts).</p>
</li>
</ul>
<h2 id="Teams" class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify" data-renderer-start-pos="10643">Teams</h2>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify" data-renderer-start-pos="10650">Within a team, there are normal members (member), administrators (admin), and owners (owner). These statuses determine the operations a team member may perform within their team. Team statuses have no effect on rights within user administration or on object or function rights in <em data-renderer-mark="true">EFS Survey</em>. In general, you can simply ignore the statuses within teams. Exception: If you wish to delete an account of someone who is a team owner, you must first transfer leadership to another team member, before you can delete the account.</p>
