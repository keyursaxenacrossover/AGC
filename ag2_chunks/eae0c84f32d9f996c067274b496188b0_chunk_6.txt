-renderer-start-pos="7970">If you have read rights for <em data-renderer-mark="true">org_groupadmin</em>, open the desired team, click on the appropriate team member, and choose the <strong data-renderer-mark="true">Send password</strong> button.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify" data-renderer-start-pos="8114">The text of the mail is predefined. In the dispatch dialog, only the basic contact data are displayed.</p>
<h3 id="Checking-user-accounts-for-brute-force-suspension" class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify" data-renderer-start-pos="8218"><strong data-renderer-mark="true">Checking user accounts for brute force suspension</strong></h3>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify" data-renderer-start-pos="8269">The admin area has protection against brute force attacks, i.e. hacking of an account using automated, rapidly consecutive entries of possible passwords. There is only a limited number of incorrect entries possible; exceeding this value will deactivate the staff account for a predetermined period. The person logging in will then see an error message, in which the remaining waiting period will be displayed.</p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify" data-renderer-start-pos="8680">By default the account will be suspended after six incorrect entries, the waiting period is 30 minutes. A suspended account can be reactivated by the system administrator (root team) or by a user with write rights to <em data-renderer-mark="true">groupadmin</em>.</p>
<ul class="ak-ul wysiwyg-text-align-justify" data-indent-level="1">
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="8912">If you have leased your own installation and you would like to have the values changed, please contact support.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify" data-renderer-start-pos="9027"><strong data-renderer-mark="true">Checking suspended accounts</strong></p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify" data-renderer-start-pos="9056">If a user reports that his or her account was suspended, or if you suspect that a brute force attack has occurred, you can check this in the overview of the <strong data-renderer-mark="true">Users</strong> menu:</p>
<ul class="ak-ul wysiwyg-text-align-justify" data-indent-level="1">
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="9228">The failed login attempts and the remaining time on suspended accounts are listed in the columns “Failed attempts to login” and “Suspended until”.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="9378">The number of logins and the date of the last successful login are also displayed.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify" data-renderer-start-pos="9464">Further details on individual login processes, such as the exact time and the IP address, can be found in the login log, provided you hold the relevant rights.</p>
