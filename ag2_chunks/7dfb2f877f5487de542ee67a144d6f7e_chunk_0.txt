<h2 id="GDPR-in-EFS" data-renderer-start-pos="30">GDPR in EFS</h2>
</div>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify" data-renderer-start-pos="44">Beginning on 25th May, 2018, the <strong data-renderer-mark="true">General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)</strong> redefines the legal requirements to conduct surveys. Users who seek to obtain insights from their respondents have to follow new rules when they process personal data relating to respondents. Some of the key changes are:</p>
<ul class="ak-ul wysiwyg-text-align-justify" data-indent-level="1">
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="343">The GDPR raises the bar for relying on valid consent for conducting surveys. A valid consent requires a clear, informed, explicit, and affirmative statement from your respondents. Users must inform respondents about the survey in clear and plain language. Pre-ticked consent boxes are prohibited and will not serve as valid consent.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="679">When they collect and assess feedback data, users must be able to demonstrate how they comply with the new data protection regulation, e.g. users must define the purpose of data processing prior to each survey.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="896">Personal data shall not be kept longer than is necessary for the purpose of the processing, i.e. feedback assessment. This implies that there is a time limit on how long personal data can be used after the survey. Therefore, users should define a retention period for respondent’s personal data after which such data will be deleted.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="1230">The GDPR provides respondents with several rights, including a right to object to any data processing, a right to withdraw consent, and to transfer their data in a commonly-used machine-readable format.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="1432">Supervisory authorities have powers to impose significant fines of up to Euro 20 million or 4% of the total worldwide annual turnover for users that do not comply with the GDPR.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify" data-renderer-start-pos="1609">If activated, <strong data-renderer-mark="true">Tivian’s Privacy Assistant</strong></p>
<ul class="ak-ul wysiwyg-text-align-justify" data-indent-level="1">
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="1653">enables users to identify (“flag”) personal data prior to the survey to ensure complete and accurate deletion of personal data which is no longer needed after the survey.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="1827">supports users to create a “consent form” to obtain informed, explicit, and valid consent from their respondents.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="1943">offers a library of purpose statements to provide adequate information to respondents about the purpose of data processing.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="2072">helps users to provide statutory information to their respondents as required by the GDPR, e.g. contact information, individual rights, retention period, and many more.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="2243">allows users to create an information sheet for respondents on their personal data which can be exported (data portability).</p>
</li>
</ul>
