 tab, you select the source question. The answers of these questions made by the participants will be sent to the <em>Voting</em> page module of Portals. You have to click on <strong>Save</strong>.</p>

<h3 id="multilingualism" class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify"><strong>Multilingualism</strong></h3>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">It is possible to send the guide survey and the e-mails in the languages preferred by the participants. You have to specify the <strong>Locale</strong> option. You have to switch to the <strong>Participants Administration</strong>. The <em>Locale</em> master data will also be used to display a portal in the language selected by users when they log in. The selected language must have been configured.</p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify"><img src="https://discoverxi-supportdesk.zendesk.com/hc/article_attachments/21015136672658" alt="Panelist Administration.png"></p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">The created survey languages can be selected. This ensures that participants receive the e-mails, SMS, and survey according to this language setting. If no e-mail template, SMS template, or survey is available in the selected language, the default language will be the fallback. This also applies if the locale option has not been specified.</p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify"><img src="https://discoverxi-supportdesk.zendesk.com/hc/article_attachments/21015136670482" alt="Panelist Variables.png"></p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">In the <strong>Template Configuration</strong>, which can be found under <strong>Project Properties</strong>, you can add the <em>u_locale</em> master data as a panelist variable in order to transfer the master data to your guide project.</p>
<h2 id="creating-a-guide-process" class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify"><strong>Creating a Guide Process</strong></h2>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">To create and configure one of the three guide types, <em>Leadership-Guide</em> (ME-Guide), T<em>eam-Improvement-Guide</em> (US-Guide), or<em> Ideation-Guide </em>(IDEA-Guide), first open the subnavigation<strong> Guides</strong> in the CMS area of<em> Portals</em> and click on the <strong>+ New Guide</strong> Button. Then select the desired guide type.</p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify"><img src="https://discoverxi-supportdesk.zendesk.com/hc/article_attachments/21015166619666" alt="Guide Modal.png"></p>
<h3 id="basic-information" class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify"><strong>Basic Information</strong></h3>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">Use the <strong>Basic Information</strong> tab to give the newly added guide a title, enter a short description, and upload an image to give the guide initiator with some basic information about your guide project. The title, description, and image will be displayed on the guide overview page.</p>
<h3 id="additional-information" class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify"><strong>Additional Information</strong></h3>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">On the <strong>Additional Information</strong> tab, you can add texts, photos, videos, and links in order to. inform the guide initiator about the purpose of the guide project. This information will be available by clicking on the <strong>More Details</strong> Button.</p>
<h3 id="categories" class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify"><strong>Categories</strong></h3>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">On the <strong>Categories</strong> tab, categories can be applied to the guide. To create a category, click on the link icon and switch to the<strong> Categories </strong>menu item (<strong>Libraries</strong>).</p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify"><img src="https://discoverxi-supportdesk.zendesk.com/hc/article_attachments/21015166720402" alt="Categories.png"></p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">The selected categories will be available in the guide setup. To assign a question to a category, you have to switch to the <strong>Questionnaire Editor</strong> of the PE project. Open the configuration menu of the question and select the desired category.</p>
