<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify"><strong>Internal comparison unit</strong></p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">The unit element <strong>Comparison unit</strong> first branches into <strong>Internal comparisons</strong> and <strong>Configured comparisons</strong>. Under <strong>Internal comparisons,</strong> you will find the <strong>Level-Up comparison unit</strong>.</p>
<ul class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">
<li>The <strong>Level-up comparison unit</strong> refers exclusively to the <strong>Focus unit</strong> and forms a dynamic pair with it.</li>
<li>You do not remove any unit manually in the organizational structure.</li>
<li>The next-higher unit of the organizational structure is used as a comparison with the <strong>Focus unit</strong>.</li>
<li>As long as no specific unit has been selected for the placeholder element <strong>Focus unit</strong>, the <strong>Level-up comparison unit</strong> is only abstractly available.</li>
<li>You use this <strong>Comparison unit</strong> to compare the <strong>Focus unit</strong> with the next higher unit in the organizational structure.</li>
</ul>

<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify"><strong>Configured comparisons</strong></p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">You use the element <strong>Configured comparisons</strong> to access all comparison groups that you create in the <strong>Report Wizard</strong> under <strong>Settings</strong>. The members of the comparison group are determined in the second step of ordering the report.</p>
<ul class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">
<li>The comparison units that form the members of the comparison groups do not have a name but are counted as placeholders from top to bottom.</li>
<li>During the report ordering process, you ensure that the desired unit of the organizational structure is taken from the members selected in the mapping.</li>
<li>The element <strong>Configured comparisons</strong> is useful if you want to make a comparison free of the organizational structure.</li>
</ul>
