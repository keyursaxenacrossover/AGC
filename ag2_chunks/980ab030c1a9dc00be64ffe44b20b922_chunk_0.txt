<h2 id="h_01HGKSMPRT0QJQX3YJVJ8XBXNM" class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">Exporting Master Data or Participant Data</h2>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">To export master data or participant data, follow these steps:</p>
<ol class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">
<li>Go to People → Export.</li>
<li>Select “Panel data” as the data type and click Next.<img src="https://discoverxi-supportdesk.zendesk.com/hc/article_attachments/21017142448530">
</li>
<li>Choose the desired export template from the options:
<ul>
<li>Master data</li>
<li>Participant data</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Optionally, restrict the export to specific categories, groups, panel statuses, or selected variables using selective export.</li>
<li>Click Export and then Copy file to local PC on the following page to reconfirm the export.</li>
<li>When the download begins, a pop-up window will appear. Click Save.</li>
<li>Choose the folder to save the file to and optionally rename the file. Confirm by clicking Save.</li>
<li>Once the download is complete, close the window by clicking Close window.</li>
</ol>

<h2 id="h_01HGKSMPRT5QQCQX9QXQJ1YJNR" class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">Panel-Specific Setting Options in Detail</h2>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">The basic and advanced options in the People → Export menu are similar to those in the “EFS Survey” manual. Here are the differences in export templates and restriction options.</p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify"><strong>Selecting an Export Template</strong></p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">Available export templates:</p>
<ol class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">
<li>
<strong>Master data</strong>: System data and master data.</li>
<li>
<strong>Participant data</strong>: Data from the participant database.</li>
</ol>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify"><strong>Creating and Saving Export Templates</strong></p>
<ul class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">
<li>Use the People → Export → Template editor menu to create and save custom export templates for panel data.</li>
<li>This is useful if you frequently export data for a selection of variables, eliminating the need to repeatedly select variables for selective export.</li>
</ul>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify"><strong>Restricting Export to Individual Panelist Groups</strong></p>
<ul class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">
<li>Export data for selected participants instead of all panelists by applying restrictions:
<ul>
<li>
<strong>Group category</strong>: Restrict to a specific group category.</li>
<li>
<strong>Panel group</strong>: Restrict to a panel group, selectable after choosing the category.</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify"><strong>Restricting Export to Panelists with Specific Status</strong></p>
<ul class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">
<li>Export data of panelists with specific statuses (e.g., “Admitted temporarily”).</li>
<li>Select desired statuses by ticking the appropriate checkboxes in the “Apply to panelists with status” row. All checkboxes are ticked by default.</li>
</ul>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify"><strong>Restricting Export to Selected Variables</strong></p>
<ul class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">
<li>Use selective export to export only chosen variables, suitable for managing large amounts of master data.</li>
