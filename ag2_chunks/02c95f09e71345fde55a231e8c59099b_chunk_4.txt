-renderer-start-pos="5195">As early as during the field phase, the <strong data-renderer-mark="true">Online statistics </strong>show you the survey data that have been collected so far.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="5316">In the <strong data-renderer-mark="true">Open-ended answers </strong>menu you can find the answers to open-ended questions given by the survey participants in a table.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify" data-renderer-start-pos="5446">Optionally, you can use the following additional functions, which will be introduced in detail in the following Chapters:</p>
<ul class="ak-ul wysiwyg-text-align-justify" data-indent-level="1">
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="5571">The <strong data-renderer-mark="true">Respondent export </strong>menu contains the so-called <em data-renderer-mark="true">respondents, </em>i.e. all those participants who actually participated in a survey.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="5704">In the <strong data-renderer-mark="true">List of the reports </strong>menu you can download automatically-generated export data records.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="5801">Furthermore, you can make result reports available in the <strong data-renderer-mark="true">List of the reports </strong>menu.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="Field-Report" class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify" data-renderer-start-pos="5888"><strong data-renderer-mark="true">Field Report</strong></h3>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify" data-renderer-start-pos="5902">The Field report provides information on the essential characteristic data of a study. It contains</p>
<ul class="ak-ul wysiwyg-text-align-justify" data-indent-level="1">
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="6004">statistical information, broken down by gross and net sample.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="6069">access statistics listing, for example, the time of days with the most accesses and</p>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="6154">the average number of participants per day and week.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="6210">drop-out statistics which show you on which questionnaire page most participants dropped out. If the drop-out rate is too high, you can react by changing the ques- tionnaire dramaturgy. Errors, for example in filter setting, can also be detected by analyzing the drop-out statistics.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="6497">quota statistics.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify" data-renderer-start-pos="6518">Below you can find:</p>
<ul class="ak-ul wysiwyg-text-align-justify" data-indent-level="1">
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="6541">information on the diagram</p>
</li>
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="6571">an explanation of the key statistics</p>
</li>
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="6611">an overview of the EFS disposition codes</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify" data-renderer-start-pos="6655">Several additional features help you to analyse and process the data:</p>
<ul class="ak-ul wysiwyg-text-align-justify" data-indent-level="1">
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="6728">Button <strong data-renderer-mark="true">Configuration</strong>: If necessary, you can restrict the field report to disposition codes or to a specific characteristics of a selected variable.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="6880">Tab <strong data-renderer-mark="true">Excel export</strong>: You can export the field report as an MS Excel file to pass the field report data on to customers or to create diagrams from the data, e.g. using MS Excel.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="7058">Tab <strong data-renderer-mark="true">Split</strong>: You can split the field report.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="7104">Tab <strong data-renderer-mark="true">Target value</strong>: On this tab, you can define a target value for the project, which will be shown in the diagram and thus allows you to visually monitor your success.</p>
</li>
</ul>
