600;">Configuration of the languages of the consent form</span>

<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify" data-renderer-start-pos="9843">The colored background of the three sections indicates whether and to what extent the sections are configured. The data retention period will turn green as soon as you specify a duration, either<strong data-renderer-mark="true"> Exact</strong> or <strong data-renderer-mark="true">Unlimited</strong>. The new “Status” column shows you which languages have already been configured. These are marked either red (not configured) or green (configured). The overall status of the consent form results from the configuration status of the individual languages. Three statuses can be displayed in color:</p>
<ul class="ak-ul wysiwyg-text-align-justify" data-indent-level="1">
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="10357"><span class="fabric-text-color-mark wysiwyg-color-red" style="color: var(--custom-palette-color,inherit);" data-renderer-mark="true" data-text-custom-color="#ff5630">Red</span>: Neither the default language nor any of the other languages have been edited.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="10443"><span class="fabric-text-color-mark wysiwyg-color-yellow" style="color: var(--custom-palette-color,inherit);" data-renderer-mark="true" data-text-custom-color="#ff991f">Yellow</span>: The default language has been configured, but at least one of the other languages has not been configured.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="10561"><span class="fabric-text-color-mark wysiwyg-color-green" style="color: var(--custom-palette-color,inherit);" data-renderer-mark="true" data-text-custom-color="#36b37e">Green</span>: All languages have been edited.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify" data-renderer-start-pos="10603">If the consent form has the "Yellow" status, the consent form is considered as configured. By clicking on the menu item <strong data-renderer-mark="true">Personal data flagging</strong>, <strong data-renderer-mark="true">Data retention period</strong> or <strong data-renderer-mark="true">Consent form</strong> you will reach the GDPR settings, where you will find an overview area under the menu item "Mark personal data", which lists, among other things, all participant variables used in the project and questions used in the questionnaire, which you can mark here as personal data.</p>

You configure the language of the consent form for which your team has write permission. You can only read the content of the consent form in the languages for which your team has the read permission. If your team has neither read nor write permissions, the created languages are only listed in the questionnaire editor without you being able to read or edit them.</em></div>

<h2 id="The-flagging-of-personal-data" data-renderer-start-pos="11551">The flagging of personal data</h2>
</div>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify" data-renderer-start-pos="11583">For each question and variable you create, you have the option to tick "Flag question as personal data" resp. "Flag as personal data". Under the menu item <strong data-renderer-mark="true">Personal data flagging</strong> in the Questionnaire editor, you get an overview of participant variables and questions that were selected as personal data. In addition to this, you can mark other participant variables used in the project and questions that have not yet been marked as personal data. </p>

<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify" data-renderer-start-pos="12080">For each question you create, you can select the checkbox next to “Responses to this question contain personal data and should be deleted automatically as specified under Data retention period.” to flag the question as personal data.</p
