<h2 id="withdrawal-of-published-projects"><strong>Withdrawal of Published Projects</strong></h2>
</div>
<p>You withdrawal published MySight projects by clicking on the U-lock icon located under the breadcrumb path on the information board of the sub-navigation field <strong>Overview</strong>. Variables can be added and removed in the data.</p>
<p>The published project will be unlocked as soon as you click Accept on the confirmation modal that informs you about possible difficulties. The changes affect splits, recodings and new tables. After the changes, the project must be published again. The instance is reset. Even if a published project is withdrawn, dashboards and views remain intact.</p>
<p><img src="https://discoverxi-supportdesk.zendesk.com/hc/article_attachments/21015359079954" alt="unpublish_EN.png"></p>
<p>If you change variables that we used in dashboards, they will be discarded. You will be informed by modal that you have to confirm before the change take effect. After the change, the project must be published again. </p>

<h1 id="the-mysight-module-in-portals">
<strong>The </strong><em><strong>MySight</strong></em><strong> Module in </strong><em><strong>Portals</strong></em>
</h1>
</div>
<p>To make the dashboards created by means of <em>Tableau</em> accessible via the exterior view of <em>Portals</em>, you integrate <em>MySigh</em>t as a page module in <em>Portals</em>. To do this, in the CMS area of <em>Portals</em>, you use the sub-navigation fields <strong>Pages</strong>, <strong>Settings</strong> and <strong>Navigation</strong> in turn. </p>
<ul class="ak-ul">
<li>
<p>You can access the CMS area by clicking on the corresponding tile on the portal overview page.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p> </p>
<div class="fabric-editor-block-mark sc-ixltIz dsUKKC">
<h2 id="adding-the-new-module"><strong>Adding the New Module</strong></h2>
</div>
<p>You have two options: Either you add the<em>MySight</em>module to one of the existing default pages or target pages, or you create a new page on which you embed <em>MySight </em>as a new module. To create a new page on which the<em> MySight</em> module will be integrated, proceed as follows:</p>
<ul class="ak-ul">
<li>
<p>On the sub-navigation field <strong>Pages</strong> click on <strong>+ New page</strong>.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>In the second step, click on <strong>+ New row</strong> and select the desired layout.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p><img src="https://discoverxi-supportdesk.zendesk.com/hc/article_attachments/21015359078290" alt="newmodule_EN.png"></p>
<p>Then the new module is added. Before that, you assign a title for the new page.</p>
<ul class="ak-ul">
<li>
<p>You click on <strong>+ New module</strong> to open the module list.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>You select the <em>MySight</em> module from this list.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p><img src="https://discoverxi-supportdesk.zendesk.com/hc/article_attachments/21015359076114" alt="mysight_module_EN.png"></p>
<p>If you want to add the <em>MySight</em> module on one of the existing default or target pages, the first two points of the described procedure are omitted and you insert with<strong> + New module</strong>.</p>
