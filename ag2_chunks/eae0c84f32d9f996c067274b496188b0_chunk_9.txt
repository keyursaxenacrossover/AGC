>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="12640">When creating a new team, you can define the owner. You have a choice of yourself as the creating administrator and the team “Administrator“.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="12785">Assign access rights to the desired areas of EFS to the new team. It will then receive 'write' rights to the corresponding area rights.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="12922">In the drop-down list “Rights template”, you can select a user-defined rights template. By default, no rights template has been selected.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="13062">A list of the teams you have created is displayed under the caption “Which teams shall receive free access to the new team?”. If you grant an existing team access to the new team, the existing team will see the new team in user administration.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="13309">Click on the <strong data-renderer-mark="true">Create team</strong> button to confirm the operation.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify" data-renderer-start-pos="13370">Now you have created a new team that moreover does not have any members and no ACL rights (except those for areas defined in step 4), unless you did not expressly choose any in step 5. Your next steps are to:</p>
<ul class="ak-ul wysiwyg-text-align-justify" data-indent-level="1">
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="13582">Create accounts and assign them to this team as a primary group.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="13650">Configure the ACL rights of the team.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="13691">Change owner of the team, in case one of the newly created users should be owner of the team.</p>
</li>
</ul>

Choose <strong data-renderer-mark="true">Users</strong> → <strong data-renderer-mark="true">Teams</strong> and click on a team name in the list. The edit form is divided into three sections:</p>

<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify" data-renderer-start-pos="14501"><strong data-renderer-mark="true">Adding members</strong></p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify" data-renderer-start-pos="14517">Click on the <strong data-renderer-mark="true">Add members</strong> button in the detail view of your selected team. A list of users in the system appears from which you can select new members. If you wish to add a certain user to your team, you can easily locate them using the “Search” function above the list of members.</p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify" data-renderer-start-pos="14799">Once located, select the user by ticking the checkbox in the “Add” column. Now just click on the <strong data-renderer-mark="true">Add member</strong> button to add the user to your team.</p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify" data-renderer-start-pos="14945">Members added are initially assigned the status “Member”.</p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify" data-renderer-start-pos="15004"><strong data-renderer-mark="true">Viewing the staff list and editing memberships</strong></p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify" data-renderer-start-pos="15052">The list of the members contains all members of the team. The list has its own search function which searches the fields “Account name”, “E-mail”, “Name” and “Rights”. Furthermore, you have the option to extend the view to include other available information. You can do this by clicking on <strong data-renderer-mark="true">View</strong> and making your choice from the available database fields.</p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify" data-renderer-start-pos="15408">The drop-down list provides you with the following editing options:</p>

<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="15542">Set right of admin: Changes the status to “Administrator rights”.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="15611">Delete from team: The member will be deleted from the team.</p>
</li>

<p data-renderer-start-pos="15674">Export user data: An Excel file will be generated, which includes the data of the selected members.</p>
