 on the return data of indi- vidual units.</p>

<h3 id="Exporting-the-Field-Report" class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify" data-renderer-start-pos="22303"><strong data-renderer-mark="true">Exporting the Field Report</strong></h3>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify" data-renderer-start-pos="22331">You can download the field report in MS Excel file format, e. g. in order to pass the data on to your customers or to plot charts or graphs. <h3 id="Drop-out-Statistics" class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify" data-renderer-start-pos="23357"><strong data-renderer-mark="true">Drop-out Statistics</strong></h3>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify" data-renderer-start-pos="23378">The drop-out statistics show how often and on which questionnaire pages participants dropped out. Knowing how many participants dropped out and on which page helps to identify problems. E.g. if the questionnaire is too long, more participants than normal will drop out. Many drop-outs on a specific page may be an effect of too restrictive filter conditions.</p>
<h3 id="Online-Statistics" class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify" data-renderer-start-pos="23739"><strong data-renderer-mark="true">Online Statistics</strong></h3>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify" data-renderer-start-pos="23758">As early as during the field phase, the online statistics give you an overview of the survey data gathered up to that point.</p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify" data-renderer-start-pos="23884">In the first step, you must specify which project data the statistics are supposed to cover:</p>
<ul class="ak-ul wysiwyg-text-align-justify" data-indent-level="1">
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="23978">By ticking the checkboxes for a specific question or ticking the “Tick all checkboxes” checkbox, you can specify whether you want to view all survey results, only a selection or the results for a specific question.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="24196">If you tick the “Show variable names and types” checkbox, the online statistics will additionally display the names of variables, question types, and labels.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify" data-renderer-start-pos="24356">The online statistics will display the following information for the selected questions:</p>
<ul class="ak-ul wysiwyg-text-align-justify" data-indent-level="1">
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="24448">the number of persons who have chosen a characteristic.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="24507">the percentage of respondents is represented in numbers and as a bar diagram.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="24588">the total number of persons who have given a valid answer to a question.</p>
</li>
</ul>
