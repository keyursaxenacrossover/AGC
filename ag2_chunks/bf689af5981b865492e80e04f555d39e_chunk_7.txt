<h4 id="Setting-Anonymity-Limits" data-renderer-start-pos="10358"><strong data-renderer-mark="true">Setting Anonymity Limits</strong></h4>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="10384">Due to filtering or quotas the number of responses to certain questions can be so low that it might be possible to draw conclusions about the identity of participants or participant groups. However, in certain use cases – e.g., in the context of employee feedback – it is important to protect the anonymity of participants. For this purpose, you can configure anonymity criteria directly as report filters in Reporting+. These filters can also be applied either to the entire report or only to sections of the report.</p>
<ul class="ak-ul" data-indent-level="1">
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="10905">Either open the<strong data-renderer-mark="true"> Report filter</strong> menu item or create a new filter via the <strong data-renderer-mark="true">Filter</strong> menu item (see above for a detailed description), depending on whether the anonymity limit should apply to the entire report or only to a section.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="11133">In the <strong data-renderer-mark="true">Data set for report</strong> section, select the desired limit value under <strong data-renderer-mark="true">Anonymity limit</strong>. As an example, we select 5 as the limit value.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="11273">Click on <strong data-renderer-mark="true">Save</strong>.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="11291">The report will now only display results for questions with at least 5 answers. <img src="https://discoverxi-supportdesk.zendesk.com/hc/article_attachments/21015136369810"></p>
</li>
</ul>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="11442"> </p>
<h3 id="Creating-Contingency-Tables" data-renderer-start-pos="11444"><strong data-renderer-mark="true">Creating Contingency Tables</strong></h3>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="11473">For statistical analysis, it can be helpful to examine whether participants' responses are related to a specific variable (or even several variables). For this purpose, using split dimensions you can easily create contingency tables in Reporting+. In our test report, we would now like to analyze the answers to the question "Hike or Bike? What's your favorite outdoor activity?" in connection with the gender of the participants. To do this, proceed as follows:</p>
<ul class="ak-ul" data-indent-level="1">
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="11939">In the <strong data-renderer-mark="true">Sources</strong> area, click on the <strong data-renderer-mark="true">Data</strong> menu item.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="11992">Drag the desired question into the <strong data-renderer-mark="true">Structure</strong> area.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="12046">Then click on the <strong data-renderer-mark="true">Split dimensions</strong> menu item in the <strong data-renderer-mark="true">Sources</strong> area.<img src="https://discoverxi-supportdesk.zendesk.com/hc/article_attachments/21015136375954"></p>
</li>
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="12046">To create a new split dimension, click the plus icon.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="12046">The split dimension editor is opened.</p>
</li>
</ul>
