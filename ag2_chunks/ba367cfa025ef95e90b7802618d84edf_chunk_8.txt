ysiwyg-text-align-justify">
<li>Public</li>
<li>logged-in users</li>
<li>Group members</li>
</ul>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">If you select <strong>Group members</strong>, a list of all created groups is displayed. <span style="color: #091e42;">Only panelist accounts meeting the criteria selected will now have access to the page‘s contents. <span style="color: #333333;">The default setting is </span><strong>logged-in users</strong><span style="color: #333333;">.</span></span></p>
<h3 id="searching-for-pages" class="sc-eNNmBn lmEGgd wysiwyg-text-align-justify"><strong>Searching for pages</strong></h3>
<p class="sc-eNNmBn lmEGgd wysiwyg-text-align-justify">Use the incremental search function just above the button <strong>+ New page</strong> to search for a specific page. You type in the name or part of the name of the page into the <strong>Search page</strong> entry field.</p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify"><img src="https://discoverxi-supportdesk.zendesk.com/hc/article_attachments/21015069281810" alt="Search function.png"></p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">As you type, possible matches will be filtered and displayed.</p>
<ul class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">
<li>Select the desired page from the list.</li>
<li>If there is no matching page, the list of pages will be empty.</li>
<li>Delete your entry and try for alternative names.</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="designing-a-page" class="sc-eNNmBn lmEGgd wysiwyg-text-align-justify"><strong>Designing a page</strong></h3>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify"><span style="color: #091e42;">A page‘s content can be presented in different ways. <span style="color: #091e42;">The default </span><em>Portals</em><span style="color: #091e42;"> package includes pages with a default design that can be changed and adjusted to your needs. </span></span></p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify"><span style="color: #091e42;"><img src="https://discoverxi-supportdesk.zendesk.com/hc/article_attachments/21015039236754" alt="Welcome page 2.png"></span></p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify"><span style="color: #091e42;"><span style="color: #091e42;">New pages come along completely “naked” and must be given structures first to add content.</span></span></p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify"><span style="color: #091e42;"><span style="color: #091e42;"><img src="https://discoverxi-supportdesk.zendesk.com/hc/article_attachments/21015039234322" alt="New page naked.png"></span></span></p>
<h3 id="adding-new-rows-to-pages" class="sc-eNNmBn lmEGgd wysiwyg-text-align-justify"><strong>Adding new rows to pages</strong></h3>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">All pages consist of rows and each row layout can be adjusted. Four layout options are available.</p>
<ul class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">
<li>Single column</li>
<li>Two columns, a narrow left column, and a wider right column</li>
<li>Two columns, a wider left column, and a narrow right column</li>
<li>Three columns</li>
</ul>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify"><span style="color: #091e42;">To add a new row, click on </span><strong>+ New row</strong><span style="color: #091e42;"> on the content area and select a layout that will best suit your purposes. This selection cannot be changed later, in this case, a new row must be added configured to meet your requirements, and the original row will have to be deleted.</span></p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify"><span style="color: #091e42;"><img src="https://discoverxi-supportdesk.zendesk.com/hc/article_attachments/21015069273618" alt="New row.png"></span></p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify"><span style="color: #091e42;"><span style="color: #091e42;">After you have made your selection columns will be displayed according to the selected layout. The order of display will be from left to right, with the left column being displayed at the top. If you choose a two-column, narrow left layout, the narrow column will be displayed first, with the wider one below it.</span></span></p>
