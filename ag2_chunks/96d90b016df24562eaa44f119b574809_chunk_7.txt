<h3 id="h_01J3N2HWAAKHHZRY1F61621451"><strong>Viewing the status of actions</strong></h3>
<p>On the <strong>Statistics</strong> tab, you can monitor the progress of the follow-up process in your area of responsibility in real time.</p>
<p>While the <strong>Actions</strong> tab shows the content of the single actions and their current status, the <strong>Statistics</strong> tab classifies the actions according to the subject groups chosen and the process status. The overall status is calculated from all actions subordinated to the respective subject areas and their status respectively.</p>
<p>The<strong> Info</strong> section shows the total number of actions, the number of subunits where actions have been created, and the average number of actions created.</p>
<p><img src="https://discoverxi-supportdesk.zendesk.com/hc/article_attachments/21017142417042" alt="image2019-5-15_10-56-26.png"></p>

<h3 id="h_01J3N2HWAAEPKZHTZN7PNXBMK3"><strong>Subscribing to Status E-Mails</strong></h3>
<p>Optionally, project management can set up status e-mails, providing regular information on the progress of the follow-up process in your unit. If you want to receive such status e-mails, open the <strong>Statistics</strong> tab and use the <strong>binoculars</strong> icon to enter your mail address in the “Recipients of status e-mail” field. You can enter other persons as recipients, too.</p>
<p>The frequency and content of the status e-mail are defined by project management.</p>
<h3 id="h_01J3N2HWAAP2KP6A4PNEE83ZEJ"><strong>Overview of responsible persons</strong></h3>
<p>The <strong>User Management</strong> tab provides an overview of all persons responsible for the follow-up process in the current unit, including their names and e-mail addresses.</p>

<p id="h_01J3N2HWAAFQM3F043GN7AC71C" class="wysiwyg-text-align-left"><strong>Instructions for project managers: Preparing the </strong><em><strong>Action Planner</strong></em><strong> and </strong><em><strong>CLF</strong></em></p>
<p>In the following, you will get to know the typical process when preparing the <em>Action Planner</em> and implementing<em> CLF</em> in your<em> Employee</em> project.</p>
<h3 id="h_01J3N2HWAAGXVWJ54KBSQ22MM4"><strong>Creating an organizational structure</strong></h3>
<p>Creating an organizational structure is possible in two ways:</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>by initially adding<em> Org Processor</em> users and their roles to the <em>Org Processor</em> and subsequently synchronizing these users to a panel group.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>by initially adding panel groups to People and subsequently synchronizing these groups to the Employee project.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>When adding data initially, we recommend adding users and their roles to the <em>Org Processor</em>, then synchronizing these data to the panel and performing the central data management in <em>People</em>.</p>
