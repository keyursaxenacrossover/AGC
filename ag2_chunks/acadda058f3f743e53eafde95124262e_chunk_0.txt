<h2 id="feature_deprecation_overview">Feature Deprecation Overview</h2>
<p>At Tivian, we continuously improve our EFS platform, which sometimes requires changes, replacements, or removal of existing features or modules. This process, known as feature deprecation, allows us to allocate resources to develop new and improved features while maintaining a world-class employee intelligence platform.</p>
<p>We carefully consider the impact on all customers when deciding to deprecate features, aiming to minimize interruptions and provide sufficient time for communication and adaptation.</p>
<h2 id="key_product_lifecycle_events">Key Product Lifecycle Events</h2>
<p>The feature deprecation process involves three main stages:</p>
<ul>
<li id="h_01J3Q08N7VDGH0B80QRRC4AZ43">
<strong>End of Sale</strong> - The last date on which a feature or module is available for quoting/selling to customers.</li>
<li id="h_01J3Q08N7VT09AZGCH1VT3SXRD">
<strong>End of Service</strong> - The date after which customer and technical support (including bug fixes) will no longer be provided. Customers may continue to use the feature until the End of Life date.</li>
<li id="h_01J3Q08N7V1MP6KW1AADVR2BY6"><strong>End of Life</strong></li>
</ul>
<p>The feature is no longer available within the platform and has been replaced by improved functionality.</p>
