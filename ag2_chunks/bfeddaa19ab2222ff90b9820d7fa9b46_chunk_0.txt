<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">This article provides a comprehensive guide on familiarizing yourself with the Content Management System (CMS) of the EFS Panel. It covers the panel website from both a visitor's and panelist's perspective, explains how to edit the panel website, and offers guidance on planning panel sites.</p>

<h2 id="the-panel-website-from-a-participant-s-perspective" class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify"><strong>The Panel Website From a Participant’s Perspective</strong></h2>
<h3 id="what-do-interested-visitors-see" class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify"><strong>What Do Interested Visitors See?</strong></h3>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">The online panel platform can usually be accessed at <span class="nolink"><span class="nolink">http://www.your-domain.com</span></span></p>
<ul class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">
<li>On the start page, visitors are welcomed and introduced to the panel.</li>
<li>Interested visitors can view further information, e.g. on the panel’s operator or the rules of participation, using a menu, a navigation bar, or links.</li>
<li>Visitors wishing to participate in the panel can open a registration form and register.</li>
<li>Visitors already registered as panelists can log in.</li>
</ul>
<h3 id="what-do-logged-in-panelists-see" class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify"><strong>What Do Logged-in Panelists See?</strong></h3>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">After login, panelists can access the password-protected internal panel area.</p>
<ul class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">
<li>Panelists are greeted by name on the personal homepage. This page usually contains a list of surveys the respective panelists can participate in.</li>
<li>The panelists can access restricted information, such as news or survey evaluations.</li>
<li>The panelists can view their personal data (e.g. address data, account balance).</li>
<li>They can take action themselves, e.g. change their registration data, redeem bonuses, or invite friends.</li>
</ul>
