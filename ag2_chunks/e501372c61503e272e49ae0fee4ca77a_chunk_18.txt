wysiwyg-text-align-justify"><strong>Editing Forum Posts from the Admin Area</strong></h2>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">As moderator you can make use of additional features for your contributions when posting via the admin area or, if required, change or remove posts of discussion participants.</p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify"> </p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">It is considered polite not to subsequently delete or edit posts of discussion participants without a good reason. You should therefore use the editing options with care and observe netiquette.</p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify"><strong>Overview of topic posts</strong></p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">To view existing topic posts in the admin area, just click the title of the desired topic in the topic list. Alternatively, use the Edit Posts icon.</p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">The overview lists all posts for the selected topic and their most important properties.</p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify"><strong>Editing options</strong></p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">If you click on the Add Post button, the dialog for creating a new post is opened.</p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">By clicking on the title of a topic, you will get to the properties of an existing post.</p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">Using the yes / no links in the “Sticky” column, you can display a key post in a highly visible position above the thread.</p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">The icons in the “Actions” column give the following editing options:</p>
<ul class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">
<li>
<strong>Edit</strong>: Opens the edit dialog for the properties of an existing post.</li>
<li>
<strong>Delete</strong>: The post is deleted after a confirmation prompt.</li>
</ul>
