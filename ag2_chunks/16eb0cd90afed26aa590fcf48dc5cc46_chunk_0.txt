<h1 id="h_01HGKSMPRS9JVGC6E9EA3DB98K" class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">Overview</h1>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">The Mail Report is a feature in the survey menu that provides an overview of all emails sent during a survey. It helps manage large numbers of emails in bigger projects and offers various editing options for email dispatch processes.</p>
<h1 id="h_01HGKSMPRSYYE7GMM7DZ0VCKXK" class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">Information</h1>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">This article explains the Mail Report feature, its functionality, and how to use it effectively for managing email and SMS communications in survey projects.</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="#e-mail">E-mail</a></li>
<li><a href="#sms">SMS</a></li>
<li><a href="#viewing-archived-mails">Viewing Archived Mails</a></li>
</ul>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">The <strong>Mail report</strong>, which is located in the survey menu, gives an overview of all e-mails that were sent during the survey. The <strong>Mail report</strong> is designed to assist you in properly managing the large numbers of e-mails needed for bigger projects. The mail report displays all mails in the outgoing queue, sorted by sending operations. You have the following editing options:</p>
<ul class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">
<li>
<p><strong>View</strong>: Allows you to display a mail that has been selected from the respective dispatch process at random with the wildcards replaced.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Release</strong>: Allows you to release and execute a dispatch process with “Hold” status.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Hold</strong>: The mail dispatch is interrupted, e.g. if a questionnaire requires re-editing.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p><strong>Delete</strong>: Mails are removed from the queue. In EFS Panel installations, this work process includes the option of saving the recipients of the deleted e-mails in a separate group.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">If you delete emails, our support team can no longer determine who has already received a mail and who has not in subsequent support requests.</p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify"> </p>
<h2 id="e-mail" class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify"><strong>E-mail</strong></h2>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">Every time you send one or more e-mails from participant administration, an entry is made in the report. This enables you to subsequently check whether, for example, a certain group or individual has already received an e-mail. You may view the complete mail (with placeholders) by clicking on <strong>Mail</strong>. In the overview list, the following information is displayed for the different sending processes within the respective project:</p>
<ul class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">
<li>
<p>Mailing: internal mailing ID. The link displayed will provide you with detailed information on the specific process.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Subject: e-mail subject. The link displayed will provide you with detailed information on the specific process.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>User: user who performed the dispatch.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Date: time and date of dispatch.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Total: a total of all e-mails.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Queued: a total of all e-mails generated for immediate or future dispatch. Queued emails may be held, resumed, or canceled.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Sending: total of all e-mails delivered to the dispatching mailing server yet to be delivered to the respective recipient(s). Please note this process might take up to several days.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Sent: The number of e-mails that were successfully delivered to the receiving mailing server.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Rejected: a total of all e-mails rejected and classified as spam-mail.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Delivery failed: a total of e-mails that could not be delivered due to trouble on the part of the recipient address.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Not reachable: a total of all e-mails that could not be delivered because the recipient‘s address or the domain does not exist.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Canceled: The number of e-mails canceled.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">If you click on the ID of a dispatch process in the “Mailing” column, you will see detailed information on all individual processes.</p>
