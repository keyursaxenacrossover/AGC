<h2 id="first-steps-in-portals" class="sc-eNNmBn lmEGgd wysiwyg-text-align-justify"><strong>First steps in <em>Portals</em></strong></h2>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify"><span style="color: #091e42;">After clicking on the </span><em>Portals</em><span style="color: #091e42;"> icon located in the navigation bar of EFS, you will be routed to the </span><strong>Portal Overview</strong><span style="color: #091e42;">. Portals can be created and imported. <span style="color: #333333;">Your new portal will be accessible only for registered panelists by default. This setting can be changed in the process of configuring your portal.</span></span></p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify"><span style="color: #091e42;"><img src="https://discoverxi-supportdesk.zendesk.com/hc/article_attachments/21015069325074" alt="Portal overview.png"></span></p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify"><span style="color: #091e42;"><span style="color: #091e42;">To create a new portal, you click on the tile button</span><span style="color: #091e42;"> </span><strong>+ New portal</strong><span style="color: #091e42;">. A new tile for the portal created will be displayed. If this is your first portal, the tile will be labeled</span><span style="color: #091e42;"> </span><strong>Portal 1</strong><span style="color: #091e42;">. Portals added after that will carry increasing sequential numbering. Any new portal comes with pre-defined</span><span style="color: #091e42;"> </span>pages<span style="color: #091e42;">, including layout and page modules.</span></span></p>
<h3 id="edit-portal-in-the-overview" class="sc-eNNmBn lmEGgd wysiwyg-text-align-justify"><strong>Edit portal in the overview</strong></h3>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify"><span style="color: #091e42;">Once you have created a portal you can change its default name and access several settings right on the tile in the </span>Portal Overview<span style="color: #091e42;">. In addition to that, you will get the information on how many users are currently online and how many have logged into your portal (e.g. 16/40 would mean that 16 out of 40 registered users are currently online).</span></p>
<p class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify"><strong>Possible actions are:</strong></p>
<ol class="wysiwyg-text-align-justify">
<li>edit portal name</li>
<li>edit URL name manually</li>
<li>View/change portal status: online or offline (You can click on offline/online button to change the portal status)</li>
<li>open the portal, access the front page</li>
<li>mobile integration, allowing usage of Portals with the app</li>
<li>view amount of registered panelists and the share of which are currently online</li>
<li>switch to the exterior view of the portal </li>
<li>3 bar menu</li>
</ol>
