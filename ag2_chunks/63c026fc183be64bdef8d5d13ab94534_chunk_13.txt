<h3 id="h_01J3FGH73WDVPJSKT388G8V4RQ">Creating a Campaign</h3>
<p><strong>Creating a Mail Template</strong></p>
<ol>
<li>Switch to Libraries → Mail templates.</li>
<li>Create a mail template for a new campaign and assign it the type “promotion mail”.</li>
</ol>
<p><strong>Notes:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>
<strong>Sender</strong>: Select from addresses under Mail templates → Mail senders.</li>
<li>
<strong>Subject</strong>: Panelists cannot see the subject, but recipients will. Use a factual, self-explaining title without promotional slogans like “Make money now” to avoid spam perception.</li>
<li>
<strong>Text</strong>:
<ul>
<li>Include a link to the panel site using the #url# wildcard.</li>
<li>Use wildcards to incorporate the canvassing panelist's name and email address for credibility.</li>
<li>Offer a contact address for complaints to identify misuse.</li>
<li>In standard panels, use wildcards #promotion_header# and #promotion_footer# to insert panelists' personal messages into the mail.</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Designing the “Tell-a-friend” Form</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>The standard panel form includes two fields for personal messages.</li>
<li>Use #promotion_header# and #promotion_footer# wildcards to insert these messages.</li>
<li>If only one or no personal messages are allowed, remove the entry fields in the “func_tell_a_friend” template.</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Creating and Activating a Campaign</strong></p>
<ol>
<li>Go to People → Incentive management → Campaign menus.</li>
<li>Click the <strong>Create campaign</strong> button to open the form for a new campaign.<img src="https://discoverxi-supportdesk.zendesk.com/hc/article_attachments/21014859659666">
</li>
<li>Enter the necessary data and click <strong>Save</strong>.</li>
<li>The campaign, now in “Created” status, will appear in the overview table. Click <strong>Activate</strong> to start the campaign.</li>
</ol>
<p><strong>Editing a Campaign</strong></p>
<ol>
<li>Click on the campaign name in the overview table to access the “Edit campaign” form.</li>
<li>This form is similar to the creation form, with an added “Status” field showing the current status.</li>
<li>Edit the entries as needed and click <strong>Save</strong> to apply changes.</li>
</ol>
