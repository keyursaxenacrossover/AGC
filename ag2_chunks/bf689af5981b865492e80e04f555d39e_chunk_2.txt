<p data-renderer-start-pos="2944">Now it’s time to fill the created chapters of your report with content. With Reporting+, you can easily and in just a few steps display the questions from your survey project in the form of tables for evaluation purposes.</p>
<ul class="ak-ul" data-indent-level="1">
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="3170">In the <strong data-renderer-mark="true">Sources </strong>area, click on the plus symbol next to <strong data-renderer-mark="true">Questions</strong>.         <img src="https://discoverxi-supportdesk.zendesk.com/hc/article_attachments/21015166378386"></p>
</li>
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="3170">All available questions from your survey are displayed. Drag and drop the desired question into the <strong data-renderer-mark="true">Structure</strong> area and into the desired chapter. To do this, click on the question, hold down the mouse button and drag the question to the right onto the chapter while still holding down the mouse button. The bar above the question and the chapter itself are now displayed in green and you can release the mouse button. You can also select several questions at the same time and then drag them together into the <strong data-renderer-mark="true">Structure</strong> area.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="3772">Click on the question that you have just dragged into the <strong data-renderer-mark="true">Structure</strong> area. The percentage evaluation of the question is displayed as a table in the <strong data-renderer-mark="true">Workpad</strong> area. The leftmost column contains the code of the answer option, according to which the answer options in the row next to it are sorted in ascending order. The two columns on the right show the frequency as a number and as a percentage.<img src="https://discoverxi-supportdesk.zendesk.com/hc/article_attachments/21015136325650">The question that you have just dragged into the <strong data-renderer-mark="true">Structure</strong> area is already displayed as a table by default. However, you can customize it even further to suit your requirements. In the following, we would like to show you just a few of the customization options that are suitable for many use cases:</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p><em>Note: If you want charts instead of tables to be created by default from questions that you drag into the <strong data-renderer-mark="true">Structure</strong> area, you can change this default setting under <strong data-renderer-mark="true">Report → Report settings</strong> by activating the checkbox next to <strong data-renderer-mark="true">Create charts instead of tables by default</strong>.</em></p>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="4737"> </p>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="4739"><strong data-renderer-mark="true">Sorting the Results by Frequency</strong></p>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="4773">In order to see at a glance which outdoor activity participants selected most frequently, we will sort the results by frequency. For means of clarity, we will also remove the answer option code:</p>
<ul class="ak-ul" data-indent-level="1">
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="4971">In the <strong data-renderer-mark="true">Workpad </strong>section, click the gear icon in the top right corner.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="5044">Select the <strong data-renderer-mark="true">Table display</strong> tab.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="5077">Under <strong data-renderer-mark="true">Display options for tables</strong>, deactivate the checkbox for <strong data-renderer-mark="true">Show code in tables</strong>.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="5163">Under<strong data-renderer-mark="true"> Sort</strong>, select the options <strong data-renderer-mark="true">Frequency</strong> and <strong data-renderer-mark="true">descending </strong>from the drop-down menus.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="5248">Click on <strong data-renderer-mark="true">Save</strong>.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p data-renderer-start-pos="5267">The results in your table will now be displayed in descending order of frequency without the answer option code.</p>
</li>
</ul>
